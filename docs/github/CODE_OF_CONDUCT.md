# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as members, contributors, and maintainers of the OwnPay project, pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

The following behaviors are expected of all community members:

- **Be respectful.** Disagreement is healthy; disrespect is not. Critique ideas, not people.
- **Be collaborative.** Share knowledge freely. Ask questions openly. Help others grow.
- **Be inclusive.** Welcome contributors of all experience levels, backgrounds, and perspectives.
- **Be constructive.** Offer actionable feedback. Accept feedback gracefully.
- **Assume good faith.** Misunderstandings happen. Clarify before escalating.
- **Be transparent.** Disclose conflicts of interest. Do not represent yourself as having authority you do not hold.
- **Protect users.** OwnPay handles financial data. Any contribution that could compromise user security, privacy, or funds must be flagged immediately — not shipped silently.

### Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors will not be tolerated:

- **Harassment** — including unwelcome sexual attention, intimidation, stalking, or persistent unwanted contact.
- **Discrimination** — treating any contributor or user unfavorably because of any protected characteristic listed above.
- **Abusive language** — insults, slurs, derogatory comments, personal or political attacks.
- **Trolling and bad-faith participation** — deliberate derailment of discussions, inflammatory or provocative messaging with no constructive intent.
- **Doxxing** — publishing private or identifying information about anyone without their explicit consent.
- **Dishonest representation** — impersonating another contributor, maintainer, or organization; misrepresenting the source or authorship of code.
- **Security abuse** — using knowledge gained from this project to exploit, harm, or defraud OwnPay users, merchants, or infrastructure.
- **Silencing others** — attempting to suppress legitimate feedback, reports, or participation through social pressure, threats, or manipulation.

---

## Financial & Security Responsibility

OwnPay is a payment infrastructure platform. Contributors have access to code that processes real financial transactions. This creates responsibilities beyond typical open-source norms:

- **Never knowingly introduce vulnerabilities** into payment flows, authentication paths, webhook handlers, or cryptographic operations.
- **Report security issues privately.** Do not file public issues for security vulnerabilities. Follow the process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).
- **Do not use contributor access** for competitive intelligence, unauthorized testing against live systems, or any activity that could compromise merchant or end-user funds.
- **Do not commit secrets.** API keys, credentials, webhook secrets, and private certificates must never appear in pull requests, comments, or commit messages.

Violations of these responsibilities are treated as a serious breach of this Code of Conduct regardless of intent.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it to the project maintainers at:

**ping@ownpay.org** *(or file a private report through GitHub's "Report abuse" feature)*

Reports should include:
- Your contact information (if you are comfortable providing it).
- Names or usernames of those involved, including any witnesses.
- A description of what occurred, with dates, links, or screenshots where available.
- Any additional context you believe is relevant.

All reports are reviewed confidentially. The identity of the reporter will not be disclosed without explicit consent.

### Response Timeline

| Step | Target Timeline |
|------|----------------|
| Acknowledgment of report | Within 48 hours |
| Initial assessment | Within 7 days |
| Final decision and action | Within 14 days |

### Enforcement Actions

Maintainers may take any of the following actions, escalating based on severity and recurrence:

| Level | Action | Applied When |
|-------|--------|--------------|
| **1 — Correction** | Private written warning with an explanation of the violation | First-time, low-severity incident |
| **2 — Warning** | Formal warning with defined behavioral expectations; further violations escalate | Repeated or moderate violations |
| **3 — Temporary Ban** | Suspension from all community spaces for a defined period | Serious violation or pattern of behavior |
| **4 — Permanent Ban** | Permanent removal from all community spaces and contribution rights | Severe violations, persistent misconduct, or security abuse |

### Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was applied incorrectly, you may appeal by emailing **ping@ownpay.org** with the subject line `[CoC Appeal]`. Appeals are reviewed by a maintainer not involved in the original decision. The decision of the appeal reviewer is final.

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces controlled by the OwnPay project — including GitHub repositories, issue trackers, pull requests, discussion forums, official chat channels, and any event or communication where an individual represents OwnPay.

It also applies to behavior outside these spaces when that behavior has a direct, material impact on the safety or integrity of the community or the project's users.

---

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are expected to:

- Model the behavior described in this document at all times.
- Respond to reports promptly and in good faith.
- Apply enforcement actions consistently and without bias.
- Recuse themselves from decisions involving close personal or professional relationships with any party in a report.
- Keep enforcement discussions strictly confidential.

Maintainers who violate this Code of Conduct may face consequences determined by other members of the maintainer team.
